## 🧱 What is this actually doing? (ELI5)

### 1. ⚙️ The "Lego" Rule (Types)
Imagine you have a Lego set. The instructions say you can only put a **blue 2x4 brick** in a certain spot. 
* **The Problem:** Usually, computers are "dumb"—if you try to force a marshmallow in there, the whole thing might just fall over later.
* **Our Solution:** We built a "Sensor" (the `RuleEngine`) that checks every piece.

### 2. 📚 The "Magic Dictionary" (Docstrings)
You write a lot of "Lore" (stories and world-building). Usually, a computer thinks those are just "boring stories" and ignores them.
* **The Trick:** The code now "reads" your notes to figure out what you *meant* to do.

### 3. ⚡ The "Power Word" (The CLI)
Instead of typing long, confusing codes, we made a "Power Word": **`magisterium`**.
* **The Result:** It wakes up the whole system, checks the rules, and opens a "Vault" where you can type in your ideas safely.
